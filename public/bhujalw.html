Groundwater Crisis in India

Many cities in Rajasthan, Punjab, M.P., Chhattisgarh, and Karnataka declared no groundwater in the summers of 2024. According to the Central Groundwater Authority, 17% of groundwater resources are overexploited.

"Overexploitation is caused by lack of awareness about groundwater conditions"
This lack of information results in incorrect borewell site selection, leading to groundwater scarcity.

Find the Right Borewell Site

WHO ARE WE?

We are deeply committed to protecting groundwater, a resource essential for drinking, agriculture, & sustaining ecosystems. As it faces increasing threats from over-extraction & pollution, our focus is on raising awareness, promoting sustainable water management, & implementing impactful conservation projects. By working together, we can ensure the long-term availability of this vital resource for future generations.
Groundwater is the lifeblood of our planet, silently sustaining life, nourishing crops, and ensuring the balance of nature beneath our feet.
The over-extraction of water drains the lifeblood of our planet Earth, leaving the future increasingly thirsty & the entire ecosystems fragile.
